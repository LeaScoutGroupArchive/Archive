The Patrol Activity Award


The Patrol Activity Award was open to all Scouts older than 13.


This award focused primarily on Patrol life.


In order to be passed under arrangements made by the Patrol Leader and awarded by the Patrol Leaders' Council:

  1. Be in a patrol of your liking.
  2. Explain the Patrol System.
  3. Show a knowledge of your Patrol name.
  4. Take part in three meetings of the Patrol-in-Council.
  5. Take part in a Patrol Good Turn.
  6. Complete four of the following activities:
    • Take part in an indoor Patrol Meeting.
    • Take part in an outdoor Patrol Meeting
    • Take part in a join Patrol activity (with your own Troop, or with a Patrol from another Troop or Guide Company).
    • Take part in a Patrol exchange with another Troop or Guide Company.
    • Take part in a short Patrol camp (for example, a weekend).
    • Take part in a Patrol expedition (for example, a weekend).
    • Gain a Collective Achievement Badge.
    • Any other one activity of a similar nature and level of achievement as agreed with the Patrol Leaders' Council.
