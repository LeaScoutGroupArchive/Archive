The Scout Award

The Scout Award was open to all Scouts aged 10 1/2 - 11 1/2.

It focused on core skills: Scoutcraft, Adventure, Culture, Health and Commitment.


When reading the requirements you can tell how this was incorporated into multiple modern badges and awards.


In order to be passed for this badge you must have held the membership award and completed the following:

    Scoutcraft
  1. Pitch, strike and store a tent correctly.
  2. Prepare, cook, serve and clear away a meal and a hot drink out of doors using an open fire.
  3. Be familiar with safety precautions for the correct use of potentially dangerous equipment such as lamps, stoves, axes and saws.
  4. Be able to perform mouth-to-mouth ventilation and demonstrate the recovery position. Know how to deal with shock, fainting, nosebleeds, stings, minor cuts, burns and scalds.
  5. Complete a simple navigation exercise involving a map and compass.
  6. Demonstrate the correct use of six knots, bends or hitches used in Scouting activities
  7. Pack a rucsac to include provisions, clothing, first aid kit, map and compass for a weekend camp.
    8. Adventure
  8. With suitable equipment complete a one-day expedition with your friends by foot, bicycle, canoeor sailing dinghy.
  9. Camp for two nights.
    10. Culture
  10. Make an active contribution to an entertainment, show or exhibition.
    11. Community
  11. Know the location of, and be able to give directions to, local facilities such as telephone boxes, police station, bus and railway stations, public toilets; know how to call out emergency services.
    12. Health
  12. Improve your personal performance over a period of one month in an activity such as jogging, aerobics, Scout pace, circuit training, swimming and keep a simple record of your performance.
    13. Commitment
  13. Explain to your Patrol Leader (or Scout Leader) how the Scout Promise and Law are relevant to your daily life.
    14. From the patrol activity award
  14. Take part in a meeting of the Patrol-in-Council.
  15. Take part in an outdoor Patrol activity.
    16. From the proficiency badge scheme
  16. Gain one proficiency badge.
